## 23. William Barnett (GSB) - Competitive Strategy, Red Queen Theory

**Current Research Direction:**
Thomas M. Siebel Professor of Business Leadership, Strategy, and Organizations. Known for Red Queen competition theory - an evolutionary arms race where competing organizations either fail or learn and adapt, which in turn triggers learning in rivals.

**Recent Book:**
"The Red Queen Among Organizations: How Competitiveness Evolves" - Princeton University Press, scheduled for new release June 25, 2025.

**Key Insight:** Red Queen evolution adapts organizations to their environment, but being well-adapted for one context makes moving into new contexts more hazardous. This has implications for incumbent tech companies facing AI disruption.

**Recent Media:** Stanford GSB webinar "Leadership and Competition: Striking the Right Balance" discussing when to encourage vs. avoid competition.

**Outreach Angle:** The Red Queen theory is especially relevant now as AI disrupts established competitive dynamics. His new book release (June 2025) is timely. The insight about well-adapted organizations being vulnerable in new contexts is directly applicable to the current AI transition.
